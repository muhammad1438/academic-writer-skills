# Journal Selection

Choosing the right journal is one of the most consequential decisions in the publication process. A well-matched submission reaches the right audience quickly; a poor match wastes months in desk rejection or misaligned review.

---

## Journal Metrics — What They Measure and Their Limits

| Metric | Publisher | What It Measures | Key Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Impact Factor (IF)** | Clarivate / JCR | Mean citations in 2 years to articles published in prior 2 years | Skewed by high-citation outliers; only for JCR-indexed journals |
| **CiteScore** | Elsevier / Scopus | Mean citations over 4 years (broader window) | Limited to Scopus-indexed journals |
| **SJR (SCImago Journal Rank)** | SCImago | Weighted citation prestige (citing journal quality matters) | Free; broader coverage; less prestigious recognition than IF |
| **SNIP** | Scopus | Contextualises citation impact by field citation norms | Corrects for field differences in citation behaviour |
| **h5-index** | Google Scholar Metrics | h-index calculated over last 5 complete years | Covers grey literature and non-indexed journals |
| **Eigenfactor** | Eigenfactor.org | Influence based on total citation network (like PageRank) | Less known; available in JCR |
| **Acceptance Rate** | Published by some journals | % of submitted manuscripts accepted | Inversely correlated with prestige for many fields |

**Critical caveats:**
- Metrics vary dramatically by discipline — an IF of 3.0 is excellent in humanities, average in biology, and low in biochemistry
- Do NOT compare IF across disciplines
- Many high-quality journals are not indexed (new journals, regional journals) — absence from JCR does not mean predatory
- Metrics should inform, not solely determine, your decision

## Open Access Models

| Model | Description | Who Pays | When to Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gold OA** | Article immediately freely available; published in fully OA journal | Author (APC) or funder | When funder mandates immediate OA; budget available |
| **Green OA** | Author self-archives accepted manuscript in repository | Free for author | When OA required but no APC budget; check embargo period |
| **Diamond OA** | Fully OA journal with no APC — funded by institutions or societies | No one (subsidised) | Best option when available; growing in number |
| **Hybrid** | Subscription journal with OA option for individual articles | Author pays APC | When journal requires subscription but OA mandate applies |
| **Bronze** | Free to read but without open licence — can be revoked | Publisher funded | Least preferred; not truly open access |

### Article Processing Charges (APCs)

Typical APC ranges (2024–2025):
- High-prestige fully OA (Nature family, Science): USD 9,500–11,500
- Established OA journals (PLOS ONE, PeerJ): USD 1,400–2,400
- Society journals with OA option: USD 1,000–3,500
- Diamond OA: USD 0

**APC waivers:** Many publishers offer full or partial waivers for authors from low-income countries. Check the journal's waiver policy before assuming cost is prohibitive.

### Funder OA Mandates

| Funder | OA Requirement | Route |
|---|---|---|
| NIH (US) | Immediate public access via PubMed Central | Green or Gold |
| NSF (US) | Immediate public access for peer-reviewed publications | Green or Gold |
| Wellcome Trust (UK) | Immediate CC BY licence | Gold OA required (hybrid acceptable) |
| UKRI (UK) | Immediate OA for journal articles from April 2022 | Gold or Green (CC BY) |
| EU Horizon Europe | Immediate OA; Plan S compliant | Gold, Diamond, or Green (no embargo) |
| Gates Foundation | Immediate OA; CC BY | Gold only |

**Plan S** (cOAlition S): Requires immediate OA with CC BY licence. Major funder signatories include UKRI, Wellcome, and many European national funders.

## Journal Matching Workflow

### Step 1: Define Your Paper's Profile

Answer these questions before searching:
1. **Primary discipline** and subfield?
2. **Study type** (empirical/theoretical/review/case study/methods paper)?
3. **Target audience** (specialists/interdisciplinary/clinical/policy)?
4. **Word count and figure count** — does it fit within journal limits?
5. **Funder OA mandate** — does one apply?
6. **Timeline constraints** — do you need rapid review?

### Step 2: Use Matching Tools

| Tool | URL | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Jane (Journal/Author Name Estimator) | journalauthornameestimator.org | Biomedical and natural sciences; paste abstract |
| Elsevier Journal Finder | journalfinder.elsevier.com | All disciplines; paste title and abstract |
| Springer Journal Suggester | journalsuggester.springer.com | Springer/Nature portfolio |
| IEEE Publication Recommender | publication-recommender.ieee.org | Engineering and CS |
| Web of Science Journal Suggester | mjl.clarivate.com/journal-suggester | Broad; JCR-indexed journals only |
| DOAJ Search | doaj.org | Finding quality OA journals |

**Procedure:** Paste your title, abstract, or key terms. Review top 5–10 suggestions. Do NOT automatically submit to the top result — always verify scope manually.

### Step 3: Evaluate Scope Fit

For each candidate journal:
1. **Read the Aims & Scope section** on the journal homepage (not just the title)
2. **Browse recent issues** — do your topic and methodology appear?
3. **Check the most-cited papers** — is your work at a similar level of novelty?
4. **Check word limit and format** — does your paper fit without major restructuring?
5. **Check special requirements** — data availability statements, ethics statements, author contribution statements

### Step 4: Assess Quality and Standing

1. Is it indexed in PubMed, Scopus, or Web of Science? (Not essential, but indicates recognition)
2. Is the publisher a member of COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics)?
3. Is the journal listed in DOAJ (for OA journals)?
4. Is the editorial board composed of recognised researchers?
5. Are published articles clearly peer-reviewed (not just "peer reviewed" claims without process description)?

## Predatory Journal Detection

Predatory journals exploit the OA APC model by accepting papers without proper peer review.

### THINK.CHECK.SUBMIT Checklist

(thinkchecksubmit.org — official resource for researchers)

**THINK: Is it the right journal for your work?**
- [ ] Have you read research published in this journal?
- [ ] Is the journal's scope a match for your paper?
- [ ] Does the journal publish articles in your field?

**CHECK: Can you identify and contact the journal's publisher?**
- [ ] Can you easily identify and contact the publisher?
- [ ] Is the journal clear about the type of peer review it uses?
- [ ] Are articles indexed in databases you use?
- [ ] Is it clear what fees will be charged?
- [ ] Do you recognise the editorial board?
- [ ] Is the publisher a member of a recognised industry initiative — COPE, DOAJ, OASPA, STM?

**SUBMIT: Only if the answer to all the above is YES**

### Warning Signs of a Predatory Journal

| Warning Sign | Description |
|---|---|
| Unsolicited email invitation | Invitation to submit or to join as reviewer with flattery |
| No peer review timeline | Acceptance within days of submission |
| Broad, implausible scope | Claims to cover all sciences |
| No clear editorial board | Names listed without institutional affiliations |
| Publisher not in COPE | COPE membership is a quality signal |
| Not in DOAJ | For OA journals, DOAJ listing indicates quality standards |
| ISSN not verifiable | Check via issn.org |
| No retraction policy | Quality journals have clear correction/retraction policies |
| Impact factor claimed from unknown source | Real IFs come only from Clarivate/JCR |
| Excessive APCs with no review | Or extreme discounts offered immediately |
| Website errors and copied content | Poor website quality, copied scopes from legitimate journals |

**Verification tools:**
- **DOAJ** (doaj.org) — whitelist of quality OA journals
- **COPE** (publicationethics.org) — membership list of ethical publishers
- **ISSN Portal** (portal.issn.org) — verify journal ISSNs
- **Beall's criteria** — Google "Beall's criteria" for a checklist of predatory indicators (Beall's list itself is archived but criteria are published)

## Scope Fit Assessment Framework

Rate each candidate journal 1–5 on each criterion:

| Criterion | Score (1–5) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Disciplinary match | | Primary field alignment |
| Methodological match | | Does the journal publish this type of study? |
| Audience reach | | Do you want to reach this readership? |
| Prestige for career | | Impact on hiring, promotion, grant applications |
| OA compliance | | Meets funder requirements? |
| Timeline fit | | Typical review time vs. your deadline |
| Acceptance probability | | Realistic given scope and novelty |
| Cost | | APC within budget or waiver available? |

Select the journal with the highest total score that also passes the quality check.

## Time to Decision and Publication

Typical ranges (vary widely):

| Stage | Fast (days–weeks) | Typical (weeks–months) |
|---|---|---|
| Desk rejection | 1–7 days | 1–4 weeks |
| First decision after peer review | 4–8 weeks | 8–16 weeks |
| Revision turnaround | 1–4 weeks | 2–8 weeks |
| Acceptance to publication | 1–4 weeks (OA); 4–12 weeks (subscription) | — |

**Rapid communication / Letters journals** exist in most fields for faster publication of short, high-priority findings.

## Resubmission Strategy After Rejection

When a paper is rejected:

1. **Read the rejection carefully** — desk rejection vs. post-review rejection require different responses
2. **Desk rejection:** Likely a scope or fit issue. Choose a better-matched journal. Do not revise the paper substantially.
3. **Post-review rejection:** Reviewers have raised substantive concerns. Address them even if submitting elsewhere — the next journal may use the same reviewers.
4. **Identify the next journal** — move down one tier or to a specialist journal with narrower scope
5. **Address reviewer comments** — revise the paper to address the criticisms before resubmitting
6. **Decide on reusing text** — cover letter to new journal: do NOT mention the prior rejection unless journal policy requires it
7. **Consider format change** — could the paper be restructured as a letter or brief communication?

**Cascade systems:** Some publishers (Nature Portfolio, BMJ) offer a cascade service where rejected manuscripts are automatically forwarded to a suitable sister journal with reviewer reports, avoiding repeated review from scratch.

## Quick Decision Guide

| Situation | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| High-impact finding; novelty claim; broad interest | Target top-tier journal first (Science, Nature, Cell, PNAS) |
| Solid but incremental empirical work | Mid-tier specialist journal in your subfield |
| Methods or tools paper | Methods-focused journal (MethodsX, STAR Protocols, HardwareX) |
| Replication or null result | Registered Reports outlet or journals that publish replication studies |
| OA mandatory (funder) | DOAJ-listed fully OA journal or Plan S-compliant journal |
| Rapid dissemination needed | Preprint first, then journal; or find a journal with rapid review |
| International / low-income institution | Diamond OA journal or APC waiver from major publisher |
| Negative results | Consider journals that explicitly welcome null results |
